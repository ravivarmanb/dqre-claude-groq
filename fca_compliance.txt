FCA CONSUMER DUTY & DATA INTEGRITY REQUIREMENTS
UK Private Health Insurance Sector
Reference: FCA PS22/9 | ICO Data Accuracy Principle

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION A — DATA ACCURACY (ICO Principle 4)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

A1. Personal Data Accuracy
Personal data must be accurate and where necessary kept up to date.
- Date of birth mismatches between policy and claims data = CRITICAL violation
- NHS numbers must be verified: 10-digit check digit validation required
- Member names must not contain numeric characters
- Postcodes must be valid UK postcodes (validated against PAF)

A2. Financial Data Accuracy
- All monetary values stored to exactly 2 decimal places
- Approved claim amounts must never exceed original claimed amount
- Premium amounts must align with policy product pricing bands:
  BASIC: £20-£60/month, STANDARD: £45-£100/month,
  COMPREHENSIVE: £75-£200/month, ELITE: £150-£500/month

A3. Timeliness of Updates
- Lapsed policies must have end_date populated within 5 business days
- Rejected claims must have rejection_reason populated (not NULL)
- Processed claims must have processed_date within 10 working days of claim_date

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION B — CONSUMER DUTY (FCA PS22/9)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

B1. Fair Value Assessment Data
- Products must not be mis-sold: BASIC policy members must not have claims
  exceeding £5,000 in a single policy year without upgrade flagging.
- Members aged 65+ on BASIC policies must be reviewed quarterly.

B2. Vulnerable Customer Flags
- Members with 3+ PENDING claims must be flagged for case management.
- Members on waitlists exceeding 180 days past target_date require outreach.

B3. Continuity of Cover
- Members with ACTIVE policies must not have gaps > 30 days in premium history.
- Premium payment failures (OVERDUE > 60 days) must trigger policy review.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION C — ANTI-FRAUD CONTROLS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

C1. Duplicate Claim Detection
- Multiple claims for identical treatment_type on same member within 30 days
  must be flagged for investigation.
- Claimed amounts identical to the nearest £100 across multiple claims = flag.

C2. Provider Fraud Indicators
- Claims from providers with nhs_registered = 0 must never be approved.
- Providers with > 80% of their claims REJECTED require suspension review.

C3. Temporal Anomalies
- Treatment dates that fall on UK public holidays require supporting evidence.
- Claim submission within 24 hours of policy inception = fraud flag.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION D — GDPR / UK DATA PROTECTION ACT 2018
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

D1. Data Minimisation
- Diagnosis codes should only be stored when directly relevant to claim.
- Member email/phone must not be duplicated across member records.

D2. Retention
- Cancelled member records must have a scheduled deletion review at 7 years.
- Claim records for rejected claims must be retained for minimum 3 years.

D3. Right to Erasure Compliance
- Members with status CANCELLED and no open claims or active policies
  are candidates for anonymisation after 7 years.
